Uranus

General Information

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is characterized by its unique tilt. Discover interesting facts about Uranus below:

  • Equatorial Diameter: 51,118 km
  • Mass: 8.68 x 10^25 kg
  • Orbit Period: 84 years
  • Gravity: 8.69 m/s²

Atmosphere

Uranus has a predominantly hydrogen and helium atmosphere with traces of methane. The composition contributes to its distinct blue-green color.

Rings

Uranus has a system of rings, though they are less prominent than those of Saturn. The main rings are named:

  1. Alpha Ring
  2. Beta Ring
  3. Gamma Ring

Additionally, Uranus has fainter, unnamed rings.

Moons

Uranus has numerous moons, and some notable ones include:

  • Titania - The largest moon of Uranus.
  • Oberon - The second-largest moon.
  • Miranda - Known for its complex terrain.

Unique Characteristics

Tilted Axis
Uranus rotates on its side, with an axial tilt of about 98 degrees. This unusual tilt sets it apart from other planets.
Discovery
Uranus was discovered by German-born British astronomer Sir William Herschel in 1781.
