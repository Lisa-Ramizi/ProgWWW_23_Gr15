Beyond what the eye sees

Andromeda Galaxy

The Andromeda Galaxy, also known as Messier 31, M31, NGC 224, and originally the Andromeda Nebula, is a spiral galaxy approximately 2.537 million light-years from Earth. It is the nearest spiral galaxy to the Milky Way and the largest galaxy in the Local Group of galaxies.

Fun facts you didn't know about space

The largest volcano in our solar system is on Mars and is called Olympus Mons.
Neutron stars are so dense that a sugar-cube-sized amount of neutron-star material would weigh about a billion tons on Earth.
A day on Venus (one full rotation on its axis) is longer than a year on Venus (one full orbit around the Sun).
Jupiter's massive gravity acts as a cosmic vacuum cleaner, protecting inner planets from many asteroids and comets.
The Great Red Spot on Jupiter is a massive storm that has been raging for at least 350 years.
Saturn's rings are not solid; they are made up of countless small particles, mostly ice and rocks.
The Sun makes a complete orbit around the center of the Milky Way galaxy approximately every 230 million years.
Astronauts' height can change in space due to the lack of gravity compressing the spine.
