Saturn

General Information

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is known for its stunning ring system. Explore the fascinating details below:

  • Equatorial Diameter: 120,536 km
  • Mass: 5.68 x 10^26 kg
  • Orbit Period: 29.5 years
  • Gravity: 10.44 m/s²

Rings

Saturn's rings are a complex system of icy particles and debris. They are divided into several main groups:

A Ring
Named in order of discovery, the A Ring is the outermost of the large, bright rings of Saturn.
B Ring
The B Ring is the largest and brightest of Saturn's rings, containing a wide range of particle sizes.
C Ring
The C Ring is the innermost of the large, bright rings and has a faint appearance.

The rings are further divided into thousands of smaller ringlets.

Moons

Saturn has a diverse collection of moons. Some notable moons include:

  • Titan - The largest moon, known for its thick atmosphere.
  • Enceladus - Moon with active geysers.
  • Mimas - Known for its large impact crater Herschel.
